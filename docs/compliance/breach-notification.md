# DailyWins — Breach Notification Commitment

*Vendor packet, drafted 2026-06-11. Mirrors the NDPA's breach article and
AB 1584 (Ed. Code §49073.1(b)(6)) so the district's lawyer sees a familiar
shape.*

## Commitment

If Sure Step Education determines that unauthorized acquisition, access, use,
or disclosure of student records has occurred or is reasonably believed to
have occurred, we will:

1. **Notify each affected LEA in writing within 72 hours** of determination —
   to the contact designated in the signed agreement — including, to the
   extent known: the nature and scope of the incident, the data elements and
   students affected, the date or date range, and the immediate containment
   steps already taken.
2. **Cooperate fully** with the LEA's own notification obligations (Ed. Code
   §49073.1, Civ. Code §1798.29/§1798.82), including providing information the
   LEA needs to notify families, at our expense where the breach arose from
   our systems.
3. **Investigate and remediate:** root-cause analysis, remediation plan, and a
   written post-incident report to affected LEAs within 30 days.
4. **Not notify families directly** unless the LEA requests it — the LEA
   controls family communication about its students.
5. **Never charge the LEA** for breach response arising from our or our
   subprocessors' systems.

## Internal response plan (summary)

Contain (revoke credentials/keys, isolate) → assess scope via the audit log
and database history → preserve evidence → notify (above) → remediate →
post-mortem with corrective actions. The append-only audit log and
single-region, single-database architecture make scope determination fast.
